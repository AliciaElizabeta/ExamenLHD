\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
The \mbox{\hyperlink{classcom_1_1thealgorithms_1_1others_1_1_mandelbrot}{Mandelbrot}} set is the set of complex numbers \char`\"{}c\char`\"{} for which the series \char`\"{}z\+\_\+(n+1) = z\+\_\+n $\ast$ z\+\_\+n + c\char`\"{} does not diverge, i.\+e. remains bounded. Thus, a complex number \char`\"{}c\char`\"{} is a member of the \mbox{\hyperlink{classcom_1_1thealgorithms_1_1others_1_1_mandelbrot}{Mandelbrot}} set if, when starting with \char`\"{}z\+\_\+0 = 0\char`\"{} and applying the iteration repeatedly, the absolute value of \char`\"{}z\+\_\+n\char`\"{} remains bounded for all \char`\"{}n $>$ 0\char`\"{}. Complex numbers can be written as \char`\"{}a + b$\ast$i\char`\"{}\+: \char`\"{}a\char`\"{} is the real component, usually drawn on the x-\/axis, and \char`\"{}b$\ast$i\char`\"{} is the imaginary component, usually drawn on the y-\/axis. Most visualizations of the \mbox{\hyperlink{classcom_1_1thealgorithms_1_1others_1_1_mandelbrot}{Mandelbrot}} set use a color-\/coding to indicate after how many steps in the series the numbers outside the set cross the divergence threshold. Images of the \mbox{\hyperlink{classcom_1_1thealgorithms_1_1others_1_1_mandelbrot}{Mandelbrot}} set exhibit an elaborate and infinitely complicated boundary that reveals progressively ever-\/finer recursive detail at increasing magnifications, making the boundary of the \mbox{\hyperlink{classcom_1_1thealgorithms_1_1others_1_1_mandelbrot}{Mandelbrot}} set a fractal curve. Two types of coordinates are used\+: image-\/coordinates that refer to the pixels and figure-\/coordinates that refer to the complex numbers inside and outside the \mbox{\hyperlink{classcom_1_1thealgorithms_1_1others_1_1_mandelbrot}{Mandelbrot}} set.
